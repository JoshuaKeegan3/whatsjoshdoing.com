# Wellington Code Camp 2026
I wanted to start by saying that I have been thinking about documenting my thoughts for a long time. So long I don't even know when the idea first appeared year at least and I have always been afraid to start because I didn't know if I would continue. 

I've recently:
- started training for climbing seriously
- gained a perfect new job
- started studying towards my masters 
- joined a bunch of new clubs
- attended a lot of more industry events (both energy and software)
- started working more seriously on projects
- and finally decided to start writing this blog

So why now? I have been wanting a place to put down some thoughts and I have started writing some of them down. But I was still too nervious to put them out there. But, I believe that some of them are actually quite good and there were a few talks at this event that convinced me to write something down. 

You can expect, irregular, unfiltered, ungrammer checked, AI free thoughts that I have had. I will talk about what I'm interested in; the future, the present and maybe the past. All thoughts are my own and are subject to being wrong. Correct me, and/or have a discussion, it will make my day.

But anyway lets get to it.

This code camp was wacky, we had memes, talks that were just discussions and a speed talk that became a locknote involving "audience folding" and "quarter turns". 

The diversity was out of this world we 40+ years of experience down to -1 (don't ask how that works). The presenters ethniciaties included  french-chinese, malyasian-maori, austrlian, american, russian and at this point you realise I will forget someone if I keep going. 

I had a blast and came away with a range of project ideas and many thoughts about how I can improve developer experience.  

Here were some notes and some interesting stats, musings and learnings.
- I came out with 4 new project ideas (I might talk about in another post)
- I listened to 13 speakers
- "There are no stupid questions only lazy"
- Just start being public, if your introverted, people will come to you
- Prepare, Plan, make a short list, then use your network to shoot your shot. Milk AI, know your worth, offer to buy people coffee
- Be self-evocacious 
- Be hopeful
- Surround yourself with geniues 
- Host innovation days
- Want to move fast, record your thoughts
- I think has raised the floor and habits raise the ceiling, but you can also look at it the other way around 
- You don't have to agree with all the speakers
- Learnings = Reflections + Expereince
