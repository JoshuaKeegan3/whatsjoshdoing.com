The AI boom is not just a increase in people using AI. It is not just a bubble of wealth that is accumulating around certain people. The AI boom is the first time in my (relativly short) life when human effort has exploded in one area. Research is booming, tutorials are super high quality and people who have never used a terminal before are automating their lives.

But I find myself asking, "Where is all the progress?". There are so many smart people working on the problem of AI full time and we are still using transformers with relativly the same components as when we started. Big innovations include RL, MoE, system prompts, custom datasets and "thinking". 

It is my believe that these were low hanging fruit. RL existed in the 1970s. We learn more with better teaching and more thought. I and many others following AI theorised all of these solutions. At some point their addoption seemed certain, however, labs didn't adopt the techniques.

I was even coding the mamba architecture which is used in 1 SOTA model - NVIDIA's nemotron - when I discovered that it had just been created.

We are still using attention, rope and rmsnorm. 

During my undergraduate, I was learning about transformers and I miss-understood attention. I thought that attention attended across layers of the NN. This I was told was silly and not how it worked hower it seemed to make sense. I took everyone at their word and moved on because I couldn't immediately think of how the learning functions would work. 

That was 3 years ago and just this year, a 16 year old made headlines with this exact idea. This is amazing, at 16 I was making guis with turtle graphics but how did the brightest minds in AI not consider applying the foundational technology, "all you need", across the layers as well.

Creativity is hard. And perhaps, inovation is harder. I haven't thought of a single other good idea in AI and I don't think I will. The top labs have been uncertain when super intelligence will arrive, because the rate of progress is intractably bound by reasearch, the process of having an idea and testing it. I stumbled across this idea with pure luck, and couldn't test it because the rest of the implementation was too merky. I wasn't close to having the data or compute required and I was told it was a bad idea. 

I didn't think of it because I am smarter than the researchers, I thought of it becuase I was dumber. We need to stop telling people their ideas are wrong. The collective mistakes people are making as they learn AI is the random monkey machine we have been talking about. This is the seed of creativity... And that is exactly what we need to bring about the next advancement in AI.
